Buildings in India under the British Rule

Many architectural wonders were built during the British Era in India. The British architecture left an imprint of legacy of the British Empire. Edwin Landseer Lutyens, a British architect laid the foundation of a new style of architecture for New Delhi, the captial of the British Empire. Between 1912 and 1931, Lutyens planned and designed the city using elements of British, Mughal and Buddhist style of architecture. Following are some of the architectural marvels constructed during the mordern period.

Rashtrapati Bhawan

  • Formerly known as the Viceroy's House
  • Construction started in 1912
  • Building completed in 1929
  • This mansion has 4 floors and 340 rooms
  • Indian architectural patterns colid be seen in railings and jaalis
  • House of the president of India

India Gate

  • Built to commemorate the sacrifice made by the members of the British India Army during Afghan Wars and World Wars
  • Names of the soldiers who laid their lives are inscribed on the monument
  • Construction started in 1921
  • Completed and inaugurated after 10 years in 1931
  • Serves as a war memorial; site of Amar Jawan Jyoti
  • Made of yellow sandstone and granite

Parliament of India

  • Formerly known as House of Parliament(Sansad)
  • Construction began in 1921
  • Building completed in 1927
  • Has a seating capacity of 790
  • Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha are located here and are in shape of a horsehoe
  • The circular structure of the building represents continuity
  • Has 12 entrance gates

Hyderabad House in Delhi

  • Was the residence of last Nizam of Hyderabad, Mir Osama Ali Khan
  • Construction started in 1926
  • Building completed in 1928
  • Now owned by the Government of India
  • Built in the Shape of a butterfly
  • Has 36 rooms
  • Design inspired by both Mughal and British architectural styles
